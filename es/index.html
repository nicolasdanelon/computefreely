Why use a free & open source operating system?


Completely Free

Most open source operating systems are completely free in terms of both pricing & licensing. There are no fees, lengthy legal agreements, or restrictive copyright protections.

If you want, you can even modify and make copies of the operating system to distribute them to your friends.

Safe & Secure

Almost every open source operating system in existence is built on the robust Linux kernel and GNU software system.

There are extremely few active viruses or malware, which means there's no need for pesky anti-virus software.

Accessibility

Different kinds of people from all over the world use and build these projects, and many are built with this diversity in mind.

Many open source operating systems come loaded with software meant for those with hearing, sight or motor impairment.

Community & Support

Almost every free & open source operating system has a vibrant community surrounding it, and with that you get a world of support.

This support comes in a plethora of forms: written documentation, forums, mailing lists, wikis, social media, newsfeeds or even live chat.

Versatility

Most open source operating systems have unparalleled hardware support, thanks to the Linux kernel –no more worrying about drivers.

You can install them on virtually any number of computers or other devices that you want.

Applications

There are thousands of free & open source applications and most are available to freely download and install without the need to browse the web.

All this software undergoes scrutinous review before it reaches you, so you can find and use what you like with confidence.

Release Schedules

Having a regular and timely release schedule is important to maintain the security of an operating system and to keep ahead of software bugs and vulnerabilities.

Many free & open source operating systems adhere to a particular release cadence to deliver updates and fixes, alongside general day-to-day updates.
